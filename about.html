About Abel.

●  UC San Diego ’25 B.S. Cognitive Science Design + Research

I research humans and design technology that respects them. Cognitive science is the lens; software is the medium.

I'm a UC San Diego new grad working at the intersection of cognitive science and digital experience. My undergraduate work focused on how people actually understand complex information — which is the same question I now ask in product design, just with a different vocabulary.

Before software, I spent two terms as an Associate in the National Park Service's Interpretation program at Cabrillo National Monument, designing and running public engagement programs for visitors of every age. That work taught me to scope under hard constraints, measure what you can, and write things down before you forget them — habits that shape every product role I've held since.

Outside of UC San Diego, I've worked across UX research, product design, and program management — Energy Ave (a gamified energy-conservation experience), Val's Kitchen (a kitchen-inventory tool for a real restaurant), and the UCSD Laundry App (a participatory-design effort with a six-person team). Each one was an excuse to ask people what they actually need before drawing a single screen.

What I'm looking for: a team that takes user research seriously, builds for real humans rather than personas, and treats craft as a load-bearing part of the work. If that sounds like your team, let's talk.
