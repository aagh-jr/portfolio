## Context

Open Tower Day is a public event at Cabrillo National Monument centered on the Old Point Loma Lighthouse. Visitors range widely in age and prior knowledge. Engagement materials need to work across that range — giving children structure and adults something worth their time — while directing visitors to specific locations and activities around the park.

## My Role

[CONFIRM: solo or team project? Relationship to NPS staff?] Responsible for designing the activity booklet and the Historical Explorer passport distributed to visitors at the event.

## Approach

- **Audience mapping:** The materials had to work for children (who need structured activities with clear completion rewards) and adult visitors (who want to learn something and feel oriented). The booklet uses a completion-based structure (stamp collection, bingo, prize incentive) to engage kids without boring adults.
- **Activity selection based on park layout:** Activities were designed around specific physical locations — the Old/New lighthouses (stamp stations), the Visitor Center (staff fact interview), Military Bunker, Searchlight Shelter, tide pool table, and guided tours. The booklet serves as a spatial guide, not just a worksheet.
- **Information from park staff:** The "Ask a park staff for a cool fact" activity is structured to surface local knowledge that isn't on any sign. This design decision involves park staff in visitor engagement and gives visitors a more memorable interaction than reading a panel.
